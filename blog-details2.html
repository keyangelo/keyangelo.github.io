Clients

By Michel-ange Sep 27, 2020

Above all else, understanding a client's needs and desires is essential to the creation of a website. To this end, the drafting of a specification is an excellent way to avoid confusion or potential misunderstandings. The specifications are a contractual document to be respected during a project. It allows the client to let me know what he expects from me during the creation of the website. The specifications describe precisely the needs and functionalities expected for the website, which I must meet.

"The specifications are a reference document, which protects both parties from any ambiguity.Here is a list of the main elements to be included in the specifications:Context Give your supplier the reasons for your request. The technical aspect of a project is not enough, a general context will help me to better understand why you are embarking on this project. Example: Why do you want a website? Who are your customers? How does the competition work? What motivated your decision to create a website? Objective Perimeter Functioning Budget Deadline estimate

Once the quote has been signed and the content received, the service provider is able to draw up a site map, a tree structure, which will indicate how it imagines the architecture or skeleton of the site. The client's content allows the web designer to classify, group or separate it in a relevant way. This site map, which can be approved and signed by the client, determines the number of pages, their position in the menu and their importance. This is a key stage in the creation of a website. Once the sitemap has been validated, the service provider will work on the design of the site, its visual appearance. This can be done either from scratch or by integrating the graphic universe of the client's company. If the client has just created his company, the service provider may be in charge of creating a logo, a colour space, and choosing fonts. The graphic designer will send a moodboard and a creative brief beforehand to frame the visual creation. When the graphic elements are known, the service provider will integrate them into the design of the site. Starting with the logo, of course, which must be adapted to a web version if necessary. The colours will be deployed on most elements of the site: The menu The backgrounds Fonts Titles and words in bold Ambiences and images website design The wireframe is an approximate model of your site, which gives an idea of the distribution of your texts, photos, videos or any other content. It is a kind of draft of the final model, which allows me to collect the client's opinion on web design ideas. The wireframe indicates the text areas, images and the form that the web design will take in general, depending on the client's content. This stage allows us to rough out the work before producing a final model. The mock-up of the site, or the home page, sets out images, colours, fonts and functionalities. It presents the menu, the footer, and the management of the site's space and content. It presents the web design solutions chosen to highlight the content. The client thus knows in advance what his site will look like. The mock-up is also a tool for the client to express his wishes and to ask for modifications before the work on the site begins. The mock-up is decisive for the creation of a website.

The referencing stage is crucial for the creation of a website. Referencing is a set of practices that will make your site visible. We speak of natural referencing to determine the position of your pages in search engines, and which concerns the work that you do on the site to appear in this ranking. And we talk about paid referencing for the ads that you can set up with Google Adwords for example, and which will bring you traffic via various sources: advertising inserts on gmail, youtube, other sites... Thinking about referencing must therefore be done very early in the design process. The site map, titles, content must take into account SEO. In summary, planning a website's SEO is about determining a set of relevant keywords implementing these keywords by structuring the content (pages, titles, texts, images) creating an internal link for the pages of the site optimising the mobile version obtain inbound links work on speed and security, avoid errors vary the content Paid referencing I can also propose to implement paid referencing campaigns. By creating specific pages and targeted ads, to reach a defined audience. These ads have a monthly cost determined in advance (e.g. €50, 200 or 1000 per month) and are linked to a service invoiced by the agency: setting up the account and monitoring/updating the ads, bidding on the right keywords. By working on these two aspects, I ensure the client a good visibility, which is a key factor for the health of the site, and which also justifies the investment in its creation! I work on wordpress and prestashop mainly. My role is to create a site in adequacy with the field of activity and the specifications proposed. The next step is the integration of the content. The content is the text, photos and videos provided by the client beforehand. It can also be the products of your online shop. I take care of integrating the content, in accordance with the site map and the model. Your content must be qualitative: create original content (not copy and paste from another site)create varied content (infographics, news, pages, services, videos, photo galleries) use professional quality photos take into account referencing in the content have a coherent graphic charter (logo, iconography, colourimetry, font) It is now time to integrate the functionalities of your site, which can be, for example contact forms a newsletter module an online booking tool extensions for speed, cache, or security a shop a forum a member area a google map an online payment solution a shopping cart reminder These features give consistency to your site and improve the user experience. That's it, the site is ready: I will (if necessary) set up an administrator area for the client to access the site. Training can be provided to help the client get started. There are no surprises for the client: he knows from the start how the site is set up and what form it will take. They have seen the site map, the mock-up, and have validated each step beforehand. All that remains is the hardest part: the billing! I offer sites adapted to the budget of small companies and the self-employed, with payment facilities and possibly monthly payments.
